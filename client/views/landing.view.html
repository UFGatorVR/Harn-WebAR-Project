    Augmented Reality (AR) refers to any computer generated images or animations superimposed onto the real world. Students from the University of Florida’s Gator Virtual Reality Club collaborated with the museum to produce 3 unique AR installations for 3 different works of art.

    Visit special wall markers at these works of art for 3D AR content:

    • Yayoi Kusama’s Nets-Infinity (TWOS)
    • Jeff Joyce’s Veil
    • Michael Light’s Full Moon (Edward White Spacewalking Above Texas: Photo by James McDivitt, Gemini 4, 1965)

    To view the content:

    1. Tap the “Enter!” button on the bottom of your screen.
    2. Hold up your smartphone so the marker image is within the black square brackets.
    3. Press the triangle “Play” button
    4. Experience Augmented Reality (AR) inspired by the art.

    To view these instructions again, press the “Back” button in the upper left-hand corner. If you have any questions, please visit the Bishop Study Center.

    Kusama's Box

    For this piece, we thought about the process of painting Nets-Infinity (TWOS). Similar to Kusama, we start with a blank canvas (in our case, a cube) and begin to add red in order to achieve the illusion of a red canvas with white dots.

    Animated Planetarium

    The prints by Michael Light inspired us to expand on the experience of outer space. The entire solar system to approximate scale is represented in our 3D model. If you want to play around with this model and the code it took to put it in your browser, click here.

    Nature Portal

    For this piece, we highlight the “appreciation of nature” theme that Joyce captures in his work. By inviting you to explore a scene at La Chua Trail, we promote exploration of Gainesville's natural landscape beyond the museum walls.
